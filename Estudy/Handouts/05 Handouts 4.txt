Basic Linux Commands

1.	ls / dir – to check the files inside the directory.
2.	cd – to enter or access the directory.
3.	mkdir directory_name – to create a folder or directory.
4.	rmdir directory_name – to remove or delete a folder or directory.
5.	rm filename – to remove or delete the file.
6.	date – to display the date, time, and time zone of the system.
7.	touch filename.extensionname (filename.txt) – command to create a file.
8.	cp – command to copy a directory or file.
9.	mv – command used to move files or directory.
10.	cat – command used to display the content of a file.
